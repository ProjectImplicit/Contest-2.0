Informed Consent Agreement

Please read this consent agreement carefully before you decide to participate in the study.

Purpose of the research study: This study examines attitudes, preferences and beliefs.

What you will do in the study: The study consists of two brief sessions. In the first session, you will read some instructions, answer some questions, and complete a few tasks such as categorizing words or pictures into groups as fast as possible. Specifically, you will be randomly assigned to one the following 10 conditions involving:

  1. Exposure to images of positive or negative people
  2. Memorization of words and categorization of words and images
  3. Rapid association of words and images
  4. Brief tutorial about habitual tendencies and asking participants to try to go against those tendencies
  5. Brief tutorial on trying to slow down or speed up when categorizing certain words and images
  6. Reading a vivid evocative story in which YOU are the victim of a violent crime and are helped by an onlooker
  7. Reading an evocative story in which you are helped by friends or attacked by rival groups
  8. Reading about membership in a sports team and interaction with teammates and opponents
  9. Adopting a certain viewpoint
  10. Categorization of words and images (Control Condition)

**All of the above conditions also involve association/categorization of words and images

After completing this session, you will be contacted again in 24 hours to complete a shorter second session.

Remember, at the slightest discomfort, you can leave the study by closing your browser.

Time required:The study will require about 15 minutes for the first session and about 5 minutes for the second session.

RIsks: There are no anticipated risks in this study.

Benefits: There are no direct benefits to you for participating in this research study. The study may help us understand how people think. At the end of the experiment, we will provide a thorough explanation of the experiment and of our hypotheses. We will describe the potential implications of the results of the study either if our hypotheses are supported or disconfirmed. If you wish, you can send an email to Brian Nosek (nosek@virginia.edu) or Calvin Lai (clai@virginia.edu) and we will send you a copy of any manuscripts based on the research (or summaries of our results).

Confidentiality: Only the research team will have access to the raw data. However, please remember that in the first session, you will provide your email address. Your email address will be recorded by the research team (Shaki Asgari; (sasgari@iona.edu & Calvin Lai; (clai@virginia.edu) and associated with your responses. Once you complete both sessions, your data from the two sessions will be joined and then your email will be discarded and your personal responses will never appear individually, but only as averaged data.

Voluntary participation: Your decision to participate or decline participation in this study is completely voluntary.

Right to withdraw from the study: You have the right to withdraw from the study at any time without penalty. You may skip any questions you do not wish to answer. We plan to use the data from participants who have withdrawn from the study primarily for the analysis of differential attrition (e.g., do men have a higher likelihood of dropping out than women?)

How to withdraw from the study: If you do not wish to complete this survey just close your browser.
