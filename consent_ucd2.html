WHY IS THIS STUDY BEING DONE?
You are being asked to participate in a research study of how individuals form impressions of other individuals or groups of people and how they make judgments about those people. This research will help us to learn more about the basic processes of social psychology.

WHAT WILL HAPPEN IF I TAKE PART IN THIS STUDY AND HOW MANY PEOPLE WILL PARTICIPATE?
If you decide to volunteer, you will be asked to sit at a computer terminal and read about a person or a group of people. Later, we will ask you to make judgments about these people. The study should take approximately 30 minutes to complete. You will be asked to participate here in the laboratory room. We will recruit approximately 2000 undergraduate students to participate over the next year.

WHAT OTHER CHOICES DO I HAVE IF I DO NOT TAKE PART IN THIS STUDY?
If you choose not to participate, you can write a short research paper in exchange for course/extra credit. Guidelines for the paper can be found at http://psyweb2.ucdavis.edu/experimetrix/paper_op

WHAT RISKS CAN I EXPECT FROM BEING IN THIS STUDY?
There are no risks to you for participation in this study.

ARE THERE BENEFITS TO TAKING PART IN THIS STUDY?
It is possible that you will not benefit directly by participating in this study. A potential benefit to you from participation is better understanding of experimental psychology. Your participants will benefit society and the scientific community by adding to our understanding of important psychological processes.

WILL MY INFORMATION BE KEPT PRIVATE?
Only research personnel will have access to your responses and no identifying information will be connected with the responses. Absolute confidentiality cannot be guaranteed, since research documents are not protected from subpoena.

WILL I BE COMPENSATED FOR BEING IN THIS STUDY?
You will receive course credit for participating in this study.

WHAT ARE THE COSTS OF TAKING PART IN THIS STUDY?
There is no cost to you beyond the time and effort required to complete the procedure(s) described above.

CAN I STOP BEING IN THIS STUDY?
You may refuse to participate in this study. You may change your mind about being in the study and quit after the study has started.

WHO CAN ANSWER MY QUESTIONS ABOUT THIS STUDY?
If you have any questions about this research project please contact Dr. Jeff Sherman who will answer them at 530-752-7586.
